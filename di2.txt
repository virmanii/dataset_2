## Directed Instructions

1. Prioritize brevity. Keep optional demographic questions to the ones that clearly inform outreach or accessibility (e.g., age range, experience level); don't add fields just because they're common on forms.

2. Use grid/table format for repeated rating questions instead of stacking them one after another — easier to scan and maps cleanly to Google Forms' grid question type.

3. Order questions so the form feels easy to start. Open with something quick and low-effort (like an overall rating) before asking for contact details or personal information.

4. Give optional questions a brief reason for asking, not just an "(optional)" label, so respondents understand the payoff and are more likely to answer.

5. Keep the scale and structure consistent throughout the form, and make sure every design choice (rating scale, question order, field types) would translate cleanly into a real Google Form without rework.
